I was born and raised in Hawaii and spent much of my childhood surfing, hunting, and playing soccer.
I also found myself often playing video games all the time.
I played World of Warcraft off and on for about 10 years and dabbled heavily in Halo 1/2/3, Runescape, and Pokemon to name a few.
Playing games as a youngster invoked many questions about how computers and networking worked.
Years later I found myself studying computer science in college and started my career as a software engineer.

Somewhere along the way I became interested in investing and personal finance.
During my senior year of high school I would wake up at 4 in the morning and lose my fathers hard-earned money trading futures before I had to get to class.
Since then, I have been studying real estate, as I now believe it to be the best investment vehicle.
I am currently aggresively saving to finally start my Real Estate investment portfolio.

I think we live in the most exciting time period of human civilization.
Being born in the late 1900s, I had the opportunity to witness an exponential burst in technology.
World travel, changing careers, and increasing quality of life has never been easier thanks to tech.
Networking in particular is an area in which never ceases to blow my mind.
The Internet has enabled online gaming, online shopping, online banking, and remote working.
I think most people are completely oblivious to the years of ingenuinity and dedication required to making that happen.
Humans are extremely adaptable creatures, and I think that most people think that todays tech is "normal."
The level of technology we experience today is far from normal, the world looks vastly different than it did 100 years ago.
While the advancement of technology also has the ability to cause great harm, I think its _mostly_ a good thing.
Whether you like it or not, there is nothing anybody can do to stop it.
The point I am trying to make is: the technology available today is really exciting, and I am happy to be a part of it, and you should be too!
